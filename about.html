About ARISTOTLE

Ethical Readiness for Responsible AI in Healthcare

Project Overview

ARISTOTLE (Ethical Readiness Tool for Healthcare AI Systems) is an educational and self-assessment framework designed to help organizations, researchers, and clinicians evaluate their level of ethical preparedness when developing or deploying AI systems in the healthcare domain.

The tool directly aligns with the World Health Organizationâ€™s Guidance on Ethics and Governance of Artificial Intelligence for Health (2021) and translates those ethical principles into practical, measurable questions that reflect real-world implementation readiness.

Ethical Principles Adopted from WHO

  • Protect Human Autonomy - Ensure informed consent, choice, and human oversight.
  • Promote Human Well-Being, Safety, and the Public Interest.
  • Ensure Transparency and Explainability of AI systems.
  • Foster Accountability and Responsibility throughout the AI lifecycle.
  • Promote Inclusiveness, Fairness, and Equity in access to AI benefits.
  • Safeguard Privacy and Data Protection at all stages of AI use.

Goals of the Tool

The ARISTOTLE tool provides role-specific question sets for AI Developers, Clinicians, Administrators, and Policy Makers to:

  • Identify ethical risks early in the AI development pipeline.
  • Measure ethical readiness across six WHO-aligned domains.
  • Generate role-specific readiness reports with improvement recommendations.
  • Document compliance progress through downloadable PDF summaries for audit and education.

The tool provides distinct question sets for AI Developers, Clinicians, Administrators, and Policy Makers to ensure contextual relevance.

Acknowledgements

This project was inspired by the World Health Organization's AI ethics modules and is developed as part of a research initiative toward responsible AI governance in digital health.

The tool is open for all users - from engineers and healthcare workers to policy leaders - to evaluate their systems through an ethical lens and encourage safe, transparent innovation.
